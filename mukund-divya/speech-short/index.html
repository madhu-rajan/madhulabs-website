Mukund & Divya · May 29, 2026

A Parents’ Speech

Madhu & Akhila

Short version · ~11 min

AKHILA

Good evening, Namaskara, and Vanakkam everyone. I am Akhila, (mother of the groom). Thank you all for being here and for surrounding Mukund and Divya with so much love on this beautiful day. Your presence means the world to us.

MADHU

I am Madhu, (father of the groom). Akhilu and I have been waiting a long time to share a few stories about Mukund!

Let me start with some numbers. Mukund was born on May 1st, 2000, at 1:23 AM. One, two, three. The boy showed up in numerical order.

Akhilu and I named him Mukund — one of the names of Lord Krishna. Small bit of family history: I thought it would be nice for all our names to start with M. Akhilu then did all the hard work narrowing down the names that matched. So I think I deserve at least 50% credit — for giving the idea.

AKHILA

I'll give you that one, Chinnu.

MADHU

Thank you, Chinnu.

The name Mukund means "the one who grants liberation." [ pause ] In the original story, Lord Krishna's birth was a midnight cosmic drama — storm, serpent, the river itself parting to let his father carry him through.

Our version was less theatrical. In the early morning hours of May 1st, a very small baby with a giant head — obviously taken after me — announced himself to the world at the top of his lungs, as if to say, "Excuse me, why did you bring me out here? I was perfectly happy inside Amma. Send me back, please."

But he has spent every year since living up to the liberation part of the name — mostly by trying to liberate himself from us.

AKHILA

Even as a little boy, Mukund stood out for his curiosity, his resilience, and his patience. The very first toy he reached for was a Lego block — which, honestly, explained a lot. He loved building things, organizing things, fixing things. A real-life Bob the Builder with a sparkle in his eyes.

MADHU

And Chinnu — you remember what he did at the state Lego championship.

AKHILA

Oh, that I remember.

MADHU

Mukund led his FLL Lego team to win the Oregon state championship — beating 120 other teams. And he did it by redesigning their robot from scratch with an innovative, unique design.

AKHILA

And he was independent from day one, literally. Madhu, remember —

MADHU

Tell them, Chinnu.

AKHILA

We had a little bike with training wheels, and the moment Mukund realized Madhu was not holding on anymore, he just dropped the bike and ran. No goodbye. No thank you. Just freedom.

But that same fiercely independent little boy, on his very first day of preschool, cried so hard and held on to me so tightly that the teacher had to carry him on her lap for the entire class. That is our Mukund — fiercely independent, and deeply attached to the people he loves.

And from the day his sister, Medini, was born, the two of them were inseparable. Best friends, teammates, and occasionally partners in crime. They still are.

AKHILA

There was the time he ate an entire container of sweet homeopathy pills, and when we asked what happened, he innocently said, "Amma, I didn't do anything. I just swallowed all of them."

MADHU

And there was the shaving incident in Bangalore. Mukund was about 7. He used to love watching me shave —

AKHILA

One morning, we heard crying from the bathroom. We rushed in. He was standing there with Madhu's razor, with a cut on his lip. And he looked up at us, with the most innocent face, and said —

BOTH

"A mosquito bit me!"

AKHILA

And then there was the Nintendo DS. He came to me holding it. "Amma, it is not working." I asked what happened. He said, "It felt dirty. So I put water on it to clean it."

MADHU

That was the end of the DS. But the start of a beautiful engineering career.

AKHILA

Take it from here, Madhu.

MADHU

Happily. The boy who once washed his own DS now works as a software engineer at Meta — Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, the entire empire. The same kid who short-circuited his own DS is now writing code that runs on three billion phones. Okay — slight exaggeration. But you get the idea.

To be fair — we had a no-phones-at-dinner rule in our home for years. But Mukund, working at Meta, is undermining all of it, one notification at a time!

AKHILA

Every notification.

MADHU

Mukund is also a perfect demonstration of how genetics work. Akhilu you gave Mukund your eyes, your patience, your meticulousness, your discipline, and your kindness. From me, he inherited my big nose with a dent and my terrible short-term memory. So Akhilu clearly won that round. My one positive contribution to this entire project remains: the letter M.

MADHU

We have always been an outdoors family. One memory with Mukund I will never forget — when you, Medini, and I scaled Mount St. Helens together. I will carry that day with me forever. And buddy — remember we still have Mount Adams on the list.

And Mukund — I am so proud that you recently ran a half marathon. You are now in the exclusive M club with me. I take credit for that one too.

AKHILA

Some memories stay with a mother forever. I will always remember when Mukund called me around midnight, when he was at a high school speech competition after a difficult day. He simply said, "Amma, I just wanted to talk." And then there were our quiet walks together, surprise visits for my birthday, every handwritten note, taking our whole family to watch the Nutcracker— moments I will treasure for the rest of my life.

From Portland to Chicago to Seattle, Mukund has carried himself with quiet strength and grace through every transition — and has grown into the thoughtful, grounded man that we are so proud of.

AKHILA

Divya — today we welcome you with open hearts into our family. Into our circle of warmth, love, laughter, and deep care for one another. You bring so much joy to Mukund's life, and it is easy to see why he cherishes you so deeply.

Mukund and Divya — marriage will bring beautiful seasons and difficult ones. It is not about chasing perfection. It is about kindness. Patience. Forgiveness. Respect. And choosing each other — over and over again.

MADHU

Before we close, Akhilu and I want to thank a few very important people who have made this day what it is.

AKHILA

First, to my beloved sister Anithu — Thank you for being my rock and for that beautiful song.

To my lovely sister-in-law Veena — I appreciate you more than words can ever express.

To our dear friends — Vasanthi and Vasu, Malini and Arathi, Padma and Seshu, Veena and Jayadev, Shanmu and Arun, Ruchi and Rahul, Sudha and Bhaskar, Anjana and Vijay and to Madhu's cousins Savitha, Kavitha, and Raghu-Soumya — your love and support have meant the world to us.

MADHU

And from my side — to Ravi, my brother-in-law — thank you for keeping things light among our families.

To Rhea, my favorite niece, and Chintu, my favorite nephew, and to my cousins Smitha and Ramya and their kids — thank you for making the trip.

And to Venkatesh, my closest cousin — more like a brother. He could not be here. Venkatesh — you are missed today more than anyone.

To all of our family and friends back in India, and here in Portland — thank you.

BOTH

To our Medini, the quiet anchor of our family — thank you for being there for us.

AKHILA

To Jey and Amudha — Divya's wonderful parents — and to her sister Sneha — welcome to our family. We are glad our families are joined together by shared values of love, faith, generosity, and community. Thank you!

Mukund and Divya, we pray that your home is filled with faith and peace, love and laughter.

AKHILA

Mukund and Divya: We love you both.

BOTH

To Mukund and Divya — cheers.
