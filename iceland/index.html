🌑 Eclipse Preparation — Aug 12, 5:46 PM

Camera Gear Checklist

  • Nikon D7000 body (single body — commit to 28-300mm mounted for entire eclipse; no lens swap during totality)
  • Spare batteries ×3-4 (cold = drain; pre-warm in pocket)
  • Nikkor 28-300mm — primary eclipse tele (450mm equiv at long end on D7000)
  • Nikkor 18-300mm — backup / daytime travel lens (not for totality due to swap risk; use for wide partial-phase shots if time before 5:30 PM)
  • Baader AstroSolar 5.0 film — cut to fit 28-300mm front (rubber-band mount). Test fit before trip.
  • Eclipse glasses ×4 (ISO 12312-2 certified — for naked-eye viewing during partial)
  • Remote shutter (Nikon MC-DC2) OR intervalometer — strongly prefer intervalometer to auto-fire bracket sequence during 90s totality
  • Sturdy tripod (the only one you need with one body) + GorillaPod for low-angle Kirkjufell daytime shots
  • Extra SD cards (shoot RAW/NEF — brackets eat space; bring ≥2× 64GB)
  • Red headlamp — totality drops light fast, you'll be fumbling for dials
  • Power bank for phone (PhotoPills, weather radar, MoonShadow app)
  • Lens cloth (sea spray at Budir!)
  • Pre-cut gaffer tape strip to lock focus ring at infinity (autofocus fails on dark sun)

D7000 bracket: 3 frames, 2 EV steps. Auto-ISO OFF.

Fixed Settings — Set Once Before Partial Begins, Don't Touch

  • Focus: Manual, set to infinity before partial begins. Tape the ring with gaffer tape so it can't slip. Autofocus fails on dark sun during totality.
  • White balance: Daylight (5500K) — do not use Auto WB. Corona color shifts otherwise.
  • Drive mode: Continuous high (CH) — for bracket bursts during totality.
  • Image format: RAW (NEF), not JPEG. Brackets need editing latitude.
  • Mirror lock-up: ON for the long earthshine exposures (reduces vibration).
  • VR / IS: OFF when on tripod (VR can introduce vibration when stable).
  • Metering: Spot — meters off the sun, not the dark sky.
  • Long exposure NR: OFF (it doubles every exposure — kills timing).
Totality minute-by-minute — single body, 28-300 mounted
5:46:00 — Second contact / Diamond ring. Filter OFF (rip off the rubber band — practice this!). Single shot 1/1000.
5:46:02 → 5:46:30 (~28s) — Fire bracket sequence: 1/1000, 1/250, 1/60, 1/15, 1/4, 1s. Intervalometer if you have one. Don't chimp the screen — trust the camera.
5:46:30 → 5:47:00 (~30s) — LOOK UP. Naked eye. Take in the corona. This is the bit you'll remember in 20 years; the photo is secondary.
5:47:00 → 5:47:20 (~20s) — One more bracket sequence + one earthshine exposure (1s).
5:47:20 → 5:47:30 (~10s) — Watch for the second diamond ring approaching. Have shutter ready: 1/1000 the instant it appears, then solar filter BACK ON immediately (or stop shooting — never look at uncovered sun through the lens).
Eclipse Day Tips
Scout Day 2: Walk around Búðakirkja on arrival (Aug 11) and find your exact tripod position. Sun at 5:46 PM will be ~25° altitude in the SW sky. Use PhotoPills app to confirm framing.
Weather posture: Plan committed — no clear-sky chasing. Climatology says single-point clear-sky probability in mid-Aug Iceland is ~25–35%; we accept that bet for a calmer eclipse day. If cloudy at totality, we stay at the Black Church and enjoy the day together.
Totality is 1m 30s. Shoot brackets for 30 seconds, then put the camera down and LOOK.
